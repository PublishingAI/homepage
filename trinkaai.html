Case studies - Trinka AI

Trinka AI writing assistant has already been used by more than 80,000 researchers around the world. Although the main beneficiary is the researcher with English as a second language it is also used by native-English speaking authors who need to check their documents before submitting.


There are four main elements to Trinka AI:

  1. 1. The AI-based writing assistant which has been built using our experience and knowledge working with more 1.3 million scholarly manuscripts. The engine has more than 3,000 language rules which increases daily as we learn from new papers submitted. It is a precision score of 92% currently. The writing assistant can work in US or British English. The author can also ensure that the article is correctly formatted by selecting one of a growing number of formatting guidelines such as the American Medical Association, the American Psychological Association, IEEE and others. Publisher formatting guidelines can also be added in the publisher’s customisation process.

  2. 2. AutoFile Edit allows the user to upload the document into Trinka AI and create a Word file with tracked changes. The author can just accept all the changes in one go or go through the changes line by line.

  3. 3. Copyedit level identification, which allows publishers to screen a large volume of research papers and give each paper a CE level score. This determines the level of copyediting required and the appropriate editing workflow (Level One, Level Two or Level Three editing) to make the content ready for publication. During the customisation process, we set the CE Level scores according to the publisher’s own specifications.

  4. 4. Technical checks which enable the publisher to ensure that the author has prepared their submission according to specified journal guidelines. This includes the following:

A. Ethical compliance checks – does the article contain any statement of informed consent (medical/clinical content), is there an ethics statement, financial disclosures, are conflicts of interest listed, is there a link to the original data used to generate the research?
B. Technical compliance checks - does the article have the prerequisite headings, an abstract?
C. Identifies keywords used and provides suggestions for additional keywords, as well as generating a summary of the key statements in the article.
D. Scope Match ensures that the most appropriate journal has been selected for submission. As part of the customisation process, Trinka engineers will create a unique database containing the publishers metadata so that the Scope Match process only checks for scope against the publishers own titles, to help keep the submission within the organisation. The non-customised cloud version of Trinka will check scope of the article against PubMed data.
E. Authorship details and affiliations, including whether a corresponding author is listed and any author contribution statements.
F. Reference details, checking the style and age of the reference used.
G. Figures and tables to ensure correct placement and that they are in the right order.
H. Clinical trial statements for medical/clinical content.


Trinka AI Writing Assistant has a variety of use cases:

  • 1. Pre-submission, as a writing support tool, helping authors to fix language and structural issues with a paper before proceeding to the next step. This could be a journal article, a preprint article, a conference paper, a grant proposal, a book chapter. Authors can either check the document in Trinka or download an MS Word file with the tracked changes and suggestions.

  • 2. Peer review triage, where desk editors checking the language and structure of the article before deciding whether the article goes for peer review. If an article score falls below a certain threshold then the paper can be returned to the author with revision requests.

  • 3. During peer review, where the paper is given a tentative acceptance subject to revisions. Trinka identifies what needs to be revised/corrected.

  • 4. Pre-press, where Trinka is used to determine the most appropriate copyediting workflow for a large volume of papers. One publisher is already using Trinka in conjunction with their typesetters own prepress tools, to accurately and cost-effectively determine CE levels for their research papers.
